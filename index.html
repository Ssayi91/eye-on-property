Frequently Asked Questions

What is Interlocking Brick Technology?

Interlocking bricks are specially designed compressed blocks that fit together like puzzle pieces without the need for continuous mortar. This allows for faster, stronger, and more sustainable construction — ideal for affordable housing and eco-friendly developments.

How much cheaper is building with interlocking bricks?

You can save up to 30–40% on construction costs. This comes from reduced cement, timber, labor, and plastering needs. Projects are also completed faster, cutting overheads significantly.

How fast can you build a home using this technology?

A standard 3-bedroom house can be structurally completed in 4–6 weeks, compared to 3–6 months with traditional methods. This speed makes it perfect for developers and families needing homes fast.

Can interlocking bricks be used for renovations or extensions?

Yes! They integrate seamlessly with existing structures. We’ve successfully added bedrooms, kitchens, and guest wings using interlocking bricks — strengthening the building while cutting costs and time.

Are interlocking bricks eco-friendly?

Absolutely. No firing means no tree burning or CO₂ emissions. Less cement and timber are used, and materials are often locally sourced — making it one of the most sustainable building methods available today.

Do these homes stay cool in hot weather?

Yes! The dense, insulated nature of interlocking bricks provides excellent thermal mass — keeping interiors cool during the day and warm at night. Many clients report reduced reliance on fans or AC.
